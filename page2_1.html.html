3. Geometry

Shapes:

  • Circle: A round shape with no corners.
    • - Example: A pizza is shaped like a circle.

  • Square: A four-sided shape with all sides equal and all angles are right angles.
    - Example: A chessboard has squares.

  • Triangle: A three-sided shape. Types include:
    • Equilateral: All sides are equal.
    • Isosceles: Two sides are equal.
    • Scalene: No sides are equal.
    • Right-angled: One angle is 90 degrees.
      • - Example: A slice of pizza is shaped like a triangle.

  • Rectangle: A four-sided shape with opposite sides equal and all angles are right angles.
    • - Example: A book is shaped like a rectangle.
  • Other polygons: Shapes with three or more straight sides.
    • Pentagon: 5 sides.
    • Hexagon: 6 sides.
    • Heptagon: 7 sides.
    • Octagon: 8 sides.
    • Nonagon: 9 sides.
    • Decagon: 10 sides.
      • - Example: Stop signs are octagons,honbeycombs are hexagons.

    Perimeter:

    The total distance around a shape.
    Formulas:
    • Square: Perimeter= 4 * side length
      - A square with sides of 5 cm has a perimeter of 4*5= 20 cm.
    • Rectangle: Perimeter= 2 * (length + width)
      - A rectangle with a length of 8 cm and a width of 5 cm has a perimeter of 2*(8 + 5)= 26 cm

    Problem: A rectangular garden is 10 meters long and 6 meters wide. What is the perimeter of the garden?
    Solutions: Perimeter= 2 * (10 meters = 6 meters)= 32 meters

    Area:

    The space a shape covers.
    Formulas:
    • Square: Area= side length * side length
    • Rectangle: Area= lenght * width
    • Triangle: Area= (1/2) * base * height

    Examples:
    • A square with sides of 5 cm has a an area of 5 * 5= 25 square cm.
    • A rectangle with a length of 8 cm and a width of 5 cm has an area of 8 * 5= 40 square cm.
    • A triangle with a base of 6 cm and a height of 4 cm has an area of (1/2) * 6 * 4= 12 square cm.

    Problem: A rectangular rug is 3 meters long and 2 meters wide. What is the area of the rug?
    Solutions: Area= 3 meters * 2 meters= 6 square meters.

    Volume:

    The amount of space a 3D object occupies.
    Formulas:
    • Cube: Volume= side length * side length * side length
    • Rectanglular Prism: Volume= length * width * height

    Examples:
    - A cube with sides of 3 cm has a volume of 3 * 3 * 3= 27 cubic cm.
    - A rectanglular prism with a length of 5 cm, a width of 4 cm, and a height of 3 cm has a volume of 5 * 4 * 3= 60 cubic cm.
    • Problem: A box is 10 cm long, 8 cm wide, and 6 cm high. What is the volume of the box?
    • Solutions: Volume= 10 cm * 8 cm * 6 cm= 480 cubic cm.

    Angles:

    The amount of turn between two lines.
    Unit: Degrees
    Types:
    • Right angle: 90 degrees
    • Acute angle: Less than 90 degrees
    • Obtuse angle: Greater than 90 degrees but less than 180 degrees
    • Straight angle: 180 degrees

    4. Problem-Solving

    Word Problems: Translate real-life situations into math problems.

      - Example: John bought 3 boxes of cookies. Each box contains 12 cookies. How many cookies did John buy in total?
    • Solution: 3 boxes * 12 cookies/box= 36 cookies
      - Problem: Sarah has 5 red balloons and 3 blue balloons. How many balloons does she have in total?
    • Solution: 5 balloons + 3 balloons= 8 balloons

    Problem-Solving Strategies:
    • Read and understand: Carefully read the problem to identify what is being asked.
    • Identify key information: Highlight the important numbers and facts.
    • Choose the correct operation: Decide whether to add, subtract, multiply, or divide.
    • Solve the problem: Perform the calculation.
    • Check you answer: Make sure your answer makes sense in the context of the problem.
