5. Fractions


Parts of a Whole: Fractions represent parts of a whole. The denominator tells us how many equal parts the whole is divided into, and the numerator tells us how many of those parts we have.
Example: A pizza is cut into 8 slices. If you eat 3 slices, you've eaten 3/8 of the pizza.


Types of Fractions:
- Proper Fractions: Numerator < Denominator
Example: 2/7, 3/8, 1/4
- Improper Fractions: Numerator > Denominator
Example: 7/2, 5/3, 9/4
- Mixed numbers: Whole number + Proper fraction
Example: 1 and 1/2, 2 and 3/4, 3 and 1/3


Adding Fractions:
Formula: a/b + c/d= (ad + bc) / bd (if b and d are the same, you can just add the numerators)
Example: 1/2 + 1/4= (1 * 4 + 1 * 2)/(2 * 4)= 6/8= 3/4
Problem: You have 1/3 of a cake and your friend has a 1/6 of a cake. How much cake do you have together?
Solution: 1/3 + 1/6= (1 * 6 + 1 * 3)/ (3 * 6)= 9/18= 1/2


Subtracting Fractions:
Formula: a/b - c/d= (ad - bc) / bd (if b and d are the same, you can just subtract the numerators)
Example: 3/4 - 1/2= (3 * 2 - 1 * 4)/(4 * 2)= 2/8= 1/4 <
Problem: You have 1/3 of a cake and your friend has a 1/6 of a cake. How much cake do you have together?
Solution: 2/5 - 1/10= (2 * 10 - 1 * 5)/ (5 * 10)= 15/30= 3/10


Multiplying Fractions:
Formula: (a/b) * (c/d)= (a * c)/(b * d)
Example: (1/2) * (3/4)= (1 * 3)/(2 * 4) <
Problem: You have 1/3 of a pizza, and you eat 1/2 of your portion. How much pizza did you eat?
Solution: (1/3) * <1/2)


Dividing Fractions:
Formula: (a/b)/(c/d)= (c/d)*(d/c)
Example: (2/3)/(1/4)= (2/3)*(4/1) <
Problem: You have 3/4 of a cup of flour, and a recipe calls for 1/2 cup of flour. How many times can you make the recipe?
Solution: (3/4)/(1/2)= (3/4)*(2/1)= 6/4= 3/2 (you can make the recipe one and a half times)

6. Time


Units of Time:
Formula:
60 seconds= 1 minute
60 minutes= 1 hour
24 hours= 1 day
7 days= 1 week
52 weeks= 1 year (approximately)
Example: 3 hours= 3 * 60 minutes= 180 minutes
Problem: How many minutes are there in two and a half hours?
Solution: 2 hours * 60 minutes/ 1 hour= 120 minutes; 120 minutes + 30 minutes= 150 minutes


Telling Time:
- Analog Clock:
Hour Hand: Short hand, points to the hour.
Minute Hand: Long hand, points to the minute.
Second Hand: Longest hand, points to thge second.
- Digital Clock: Displays time using numbers.
